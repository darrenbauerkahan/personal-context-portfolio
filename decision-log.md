# Decision Log

## How I Make Decisions

I talk it through with people. I bounce ideas off key folks, get their perspective, and weigh that against what I already know or can learn. I'm not the type to decide in isolation.

## What I Need Before Deciding

- What options exist
- Trade-offs for each option
- Whether we've forgotten anything or are taking shortcuts
- Cost implications
- Answers to any important open questions — if those are unanswered, I'll hold the decision until they are

## When Information Is Incomplete

I rely on experience, the input of the people around me, and whatever I can find to steer toward a risk/reward compromise that makes sense. I won't freeze — I'll decide with what I have, but I want to have exhausted the reasonable ways to get more information first.

## Recent Decision Examples

### AI Immersion Sprint — dropping the "visitor" pattern
**Context:** For the upcoming AI learning sprint, the team proposed having people "visit" other teams to expose gaps in context engineering — could a new person make progress with just the AI scaffolding? As we discussed it, it became clear that each visitor would need a full dev environment setup for the new team, which could eat most of the allocated time.

**How I decided:** I own the overall goals for the sprint and knew the visitor pattern was nice-to-have. The real value I wanted was for everybody to experience full agentic software development. I made the call to skip the visitor idea entirely and asked the team for ideas on what we could do with that time to better meet the core objective.

**Pattern:** Weighed a secondary goal against the primary objective, recognized the execution risk, and cut the secondary goal to protect the primary one.

### India hiring — approving two roles despite future headcount uncertainty
**Context:** Vivek requested approval for two hires. Both were within budget and the roles had been open for some time. The hard part was whether we should be hiring at all when AI efficiencies will likely mean fewer developers in the future.

**How I decided:** I considered multiple angles — the budget was clear, the roles were needed now, and declining would send a signal in India I wasn't prepared to send. I also reasoned that our skillset now includes AI, so new hires with AI capability are more likely to be retained even if I cut staff later. Approved both.

**Pattern:** Weighed the immediate operational need and the organizational signal against the longer-term headcount uncertainty, and found a rationale that made the decision defensible in both timeframes.
