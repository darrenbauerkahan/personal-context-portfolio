## Higher Ed / EdTech Industry Context

An outsider wouldn't know these, but they shape everything:

- **Buying is slow and painful.** Customers can't just purchase — they go through internal procurement, security review, and budgeting hurdles. RFPs are common and it can take months to close a deal even after they've decided they want us.
- **Strict compliance requirements.** We must meet security, compliance, and accessibility standards (VPAT, etc.) because our customers have to meet those same standards themselves.
- **Sensitive data.** Customer data cannot be used internally or exposed to tools. This is a hard constraint on how we adopt new technology.
- **Campus processes are slow and people-driven.** Replacing them is as much about change management with people as it is about the software we build.
- **Administrative, not classroom.** Our software focuses on the administrative side of the school — but all of it is ultimately in service to students.

## How I Frame Problems

I always think about the customer perspective first. That's the lens for every decision — how does this affect or look to the customer?
