# Goals and Priorities

## This Quarter (Q2 2026)

1. **AI-first dev stack for established teams.** Every team using agentic coding, AI features in products, no more manual testing. Success = faster feature throughput with similar or better quality.
2. **New AI-native product launched.** The new Pod team ships a product with GTM motion incorporated, possibly experimenting with new purchase models (try before you buy, etc.).
3. **AI readiness evaluation.** Assess each person in the org for AI readiness in preparation for a Q3 reorganization.
4. **Improved NIST stance.** Design a security posture that greases the purchase process for customers — not full compliance, but enough to unblock deals.
5. **Board and CEO responsiveness.** Respond to any and all requests from the board and Erin. This is always on.

## Longer Term

Full AI transformation of the company and the product suite. The team and I are AI-first, producing more software than we know how to handle, discovering new bottlenecks in our process and company, and I spend less time searching for information.

## How I Think About Tradeoffs

- **Speed vs. quality:** Never trade quality, but will trade depth of features for speed.
- **Short-term vs. long-term:** Will make short-term tradeoffs as long as they don't compromise long-term positioning.
- **Growth vs. stability:** For now, stability wins — keeping established customers happy takes priority. Growth may have to take a back seat for the established suite. To protect growth efforts, I'm carving out dedicated teams that are shielded from the stability demands.

## What I'm NOT Prioritizing

- **Full NIST compliance** — too expensive and too slow. Pursuing an improved stance, not the full push.
- **Full rewrite of existing software** — not convinced we can pull it off successfully.
- **Data-layer product integration** — alternative solutions exist, so this doesn't justify the investment.
- **AI cost optimization** — I want to get the most out of Claude and the associated tools first. Will optimize spending later.
