# Role and Responsibilities

## Core Responsibilities

Everything in the product and technology organization ultimately rolls up to me — shipping features and bug fixes, security posture, AI transformation, hiring, employee retention, and budget. It comes with the C-suite territory.

## Weekly Cadences

- **Direct report 1:1s:** Six weekly 1:1s, most running an hour. I use the Manager Tools O3 format — they go first, I probe on projects, people, blockers, and career growth as needed. I keep written notes. If I run out of time, I know I can find them easily for follow-ups.
- **Peer 1:1s:** Rotating meetings with C-suite peers (COO, CRO, HR, CFO, CCO). Each person at least once a month.
- **CEO 1:1:** Weekly with Erin Shy, though it frequently gets moved or cut from an hour to 30 minutes.
- **SVP Product (Brian Robinson):** Near-daily ad-hoc communication. We stay in extremely close contact.
- **Sprint reviews:** Watching two-week progress demos from several agile teams.
- **Staff meeting:** My own leadership team meeting.
- **Executive staff meeting:** Full C-suite.
- **Skip levels:** Meetings with people deeper in the org. I always report back to their manager on what we discussed.
- **Security calls:** Weekly, tied to an ongoing effort to continuously improve security posture.
- **AI Transformation calls:** Weekly, a company initiative I'm leading.

## Monthly and Quarterly Rhythms

- **Monthly:** Internal operations reviews and company all-hands meetings.
- **Quarterly:** Board meeting — drives the whole business to some extent. I'm often on the hook for the board's "special topic."
- **Quarterly:** PI planning for the SAFe implementation (likely to change significantly as the org transforms to an AI-first dev cycle).
- **Q2 and Q4:** Biggest selling seasons.
- **Q3 (June):** Customer conference requiring a splashy product release or announcement.

## Key Decisions

- Budget requests — approving or denying spend.
- Product roadmap trade-offs — informing what gets built and what doesn't.
- Project go/no-go — I sit on internal governance boards for AI projects and other steering committees.
- Security — prioritization and cost trade-offs for security projects.
- Infrastructure — trade-off decisions on infrastructure investments.
- Costs — hosting costs, software purchases, and related spend.

## What I Produce

- **Communication** — the biggest output. Keeping peers informed, getting people on board for change, correcting course when something isn't going the way it needs to. This runs through the entire org and across the company.
- **Decks and presentations** — for the company, my org, the board, and investors.
- **Plans** — strategic and operational.
- **Documents** — occasionally, though I tend to start them, regret it, and hand them off to someone else to finish.

## Reporting Structure

- **Reports to:** Erin Shy, CEO
- **Direct reports:** Six leaders, including SVP Product Brian Robinson
